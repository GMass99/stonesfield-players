Our Story

About the Players

Over 70 years of bringing drama to the heart of a small Oxfordshire village.

A Village Tradition Since 1953

The Stonesfield Players were formed in the village of Stonesfield, near Witney, in 1953. For over seven decades we've been entertaining audiences in West Oxfordshire with productions ranging from Shakespearean classics to original one-act plays, from beloved pantomimes to modern drama.

Most years there has been at least one production, and they have ranged from farce to Shakespeare, from panto to drama. In more recent years, several of our productions have been written especially for us by members of the Players.

We perform at Stonesfield Village Hall — a warm, intimate venue that brings audiences right into the heart of the action.

Notable Members

Some Familiar Names

🎞

Rupert Friend

Best known for Homeland and Hitman: Agent 47, Rupert was a member of the Players throughout his teenage years before going on to a stellar film and TV career.

🎬

Sally Wainwright

The acclaimed writer and director has been a member of the Players, directing several productions over the years. We even premiered one of her one-act plays in the 1990s.

🎧

Becki Sherlaw-Johnson

Our longest-serving member, Becki joined as a teenager in 1975 and has been a driving force behind the group — acting, directing and serving as secretary.

Our History

The Beginning

The Stonesfield Players are founded in the village of Stonesfield, near Witney, Oxfordshire.

Growing Membership

Becki Sherlaw-Johnson joins as a teenager — she would go on to become the group's longest-serving member.

Original Works

The Players premiere a one-act play by Sally Wainwright and begin producing more original work written by members.
